Choropleth maps allow you to see the specific counties that are next to one another through time. As you slide the bar, you can see visually if there may be relationships between counties that might have to do with environmental factors or may indicate regional or legal similarities that are different than other counties. As you click on each county plot a popup will give more detailed data for that county.

Observations

A couple observations:

  • While there seems to be little consistency between adjacent counties, Washington county and Multnomah county seem to be closely related.
  • Though not adjacent (separated by Clark county which includes Vancouver), Columbia county and Skamania county seem to change in step.
While Skamania and Columbia are not connected to each other, they do both share a large section of their county line with the Columbia river. With Vancouver separating them, could there be a connection from a shared water source? Would Clark county by not mirroring them imply that there is no connection? Or could there be a difference in how each county deals with water that might account for this difference? What other maps might be useful to underlay the data?
